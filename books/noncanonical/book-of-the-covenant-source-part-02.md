# Book of the Covenant — source part 02

## Source note

This part is based primarily on the English witness in `book-of-the-covenant-combined.md`.
The local Ethiopic PDF OCR is used only for secondary structure checks.
This remains a hybrid working witness, not a strict diplomatic transcription of the PDF.

## English witness

## Chapter 2

And Peter and John answered and said unto Him: Tell us, O our Lord, the signs of the end, and all the deeds which shall then be done by them that live in this world, so that we also may make them known to them who believe in Thy Name in all the nations, that those generations may observe them and live.

But Jesus answered and said: I will tell you some things about the end.

We answered and said: Yea, O our Lord; but now we desire to know the deeds which are the signs of the end of this world, if our Lord hath judged that this is fitting for us to know—for us, and for those who shall hear.

## Chapter 3

And Jesus answered and said: In the time when I was in the world, I spoke unto you before I should be glorified, of signs that the end is near, thus: that there shall be on earth famines and pestilences, tumults, and commotions, risings of nations against nations, and those other things whereof I have told you. But I commanded you to watch and pray.

And now hear, ye children of the light; for My Father who hath sent Me to His inheritance hath predetermined in His foreknowledge, that in the last days, out of the latest generation, there should be vessels holy, and honoured, and elect.

Wherefore I make known unto you exactly what are the things which are about to be, and when he shall arise, that Son of Perdition, the Enemy, the Adversary, and what he is like.

## Chapter 4

There shall, then, be signs when the kingdom is approaching such as these. After the famines and pestilences and tumults among the nations, then there shall rule, and rise to power, princes who love money, who hate the truth, who kill their brethren, liars, haters of the faithful, proud, lovers of gold, allied by relationship but not allied in counsel, for they wish each to destroy the life of his fellow.

But there shall be in their hosts great affliction, and flight, and bloodshed.
